# Source 5: Vowel Articulation Guide (Mouth Shapes and Tongue Positions)

Korean vowels are monophthongs that remain stable and do not glide during pronunciation, unlike English vowels which frequently morph into diphthongs. This section provides detailed anatomical guidelines for teaching the four trickiest vowels to foreigners: ㅓ (eo), ㅗ (o), ㅜ (u), and ㅡ (eu).

### 1. Vowel Properties Comparison

*   **ㅓ (eo) - Open-Mid Back Unrounded Vowel [ʌ]:**
    *   **Mouth Shape:** Relaxed and widely open vertically. The lips are flat and unrounded (neutral position).
    *   **Tongue Position:** The tongue sits low in the back of the mouth, creating a wide cavernous space inside the oral cavity.
    *   **Common Error:** Learners often round their lips, making it sound like "oh". Ensure the mouth stays vertically open without lip rounding.
*   **ㅗ (o) - Close-Mid Back Rounded Vowel [o]:**
    *   **Mouth Shape:** The lips must be actively rounded into a tight, circular shape (like holding a straw).
    *   **Tongue Position:** The tongue is raised to a mid-high position in the back of the mouth.
    *   **Common Error:** Learners fail to purse their lips tightly enough, resulting in a lazy sound close to "uh". Emphasize the tight round circle.
*   **ㅜ (u) - Close Back Rounded Vowel [u]:**
    *   **Mouth Shape:** The lips are rounded even more tightly and pushed forward, similar to the "oo" sound in the English word "boot".
    *   **Tongue Position:** The tongue is raised high toward the back of the soft palate.
*   **ㅡ (eu) - Close Back Unrounded Vowel [ɯ]:**
    *   **Mouth Shape:** The lips are spread wide horizontally as if putting on a forced smile. The teeth are kept close together, but not touching. Crucially, **the lips must not be rounded**.
    *   **Tongue Position:** The tongue is placed high in the back of the mouth, similar to the position for ㅜ (u), but with the lips flattened.
    *   **Teaching Hint:** Tell the student to try to pronounce the English "oo" sound (as in "zoom"), but while keeping their lips flat and wide in a smile.

### 2. Practical Classroom Strategies

1.  **The Mirror Check:**
    *   Learners should look at their mouth in a mirror to visually verify lip rounding.
    *   Round lips are required only for **ㅗ** and **ㅜ**.
    *   Flat/spread lips are required for **ㅓ** and **ㅡ**.
2.  **The Jaw Drop Technique:**
    *   To prevent learners from pronouncing ㅓ as ㅗ, have them place their index finger on their chin and force their jaw to drop down when saying ㅓ. This physical cue prevents the lips from rounding.
