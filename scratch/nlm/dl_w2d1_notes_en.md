# Study Guide: Hangeul Basic Consonants and Syllable Assembly

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the foundational principles of the Hangeul writing system, focusing on the formation of basic consonants and the mechanical rules of syllable assembly.

---

## I. Core Concepts: The Origin and Design of Consonants

Hangeul consonants are designed based on two primary linguistic principles: the **Principle of Shape** and the **Stroke-adding Principle**.

### 1. The Principle of Shape
The five basic consonants were modeled after the shapes of the speech organs (the mouth, tongue, and throat) as they appear when producing specific sounds.

| Letter | Sound | Physical Representation | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ㄱ** | 'g' | Root of the tongue | Represents the tongue blocking the throat; sounds near the back teeth. |
| **ㄴ** | 'n' | Tongue tip | Represents the tongue touching the upper gum/front roof of the mouth. |
| **ㅁ** | 'm' | Closed lips | Represents the shape of the two lips meeting. |
| **ㅅ** | 's' | Tooth | Represents the shape of a tooth; air slips between the teeth. |
| **ㅇ** | silent/'ng' | Throat | Represents the round shape of the throat. |

### 2. The Stroke-adding Principle
Hangeul expands its character set by adding strokes to the basic letters to represent sounds that are stronger, clearer, or firmer.

*   **From ㄴ (n):** Adding a stroke creates **ㄷ**.
*   **From ㅁ (m):** Adding a stroke creates **ㅂ**.
*   **From ㅅ (s):** Adding a stroke creates **ㅈ**.
*   **From ㅇ (silent):** Adding a stroke creates **ㅎ**.
*   **The Rolling Sound:** The letter **ㄹ** is also part of the basic set.

**The Ten Basic Consonants:** ㄱ, ㄴ, ㄷ, ㄹ, ㅁ, ㅂ, ㅅ, ㅇ, ㅈ, ㅎ.

## II. Syllable Assembly

Hangeul letters are not written in a linear sequence like the Roman alphabet; instead, they are gathered into "syllable blocks."

### 1. Structure of a Block
A syllable block consists of a first sound (consonant) and a middle sound (vowel).
*   **Example:** Putting the first sound 'ㄱ' with the middle sound 'ㅏ' results in the block **'가'**.

### 2. Vowel Placement Rules
The position of the vowel relative to the consonant depends on the vowel's shape:

*   **Upright Vowels (ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅣ):** These are placed to the **right** of the consonant.
    *   *Examples:* 나 (na), 머 (meo), 디 (di).
*   **Flat Vowels (ㅗ, ㅜ, ㅡ):** These are placed **below** the consonant.
    *   *Examples:* 고 (go), 누 (nu), 드 (deu).

### 3. Open Syllables
An **open syllable** is a syllable block that does not have a final consonant. The sound remains "open."

## V. Glossary of Important Terms

*   **First Sound:** The initial consonant in a Hangeul syllable block.
*   **Flat Vowels:** Vowels with a horizontal orientation (e.g., ㅗ, ㅜ, ㅡ) that are positioned beneath the consonant.
*   **Middle Sound:** The vowel component of a Hangeul syllable block.
*   **Open Syllable:** A syllable that ends in a vowel, having no final consonant.
*   **Principle of Shape:** The design philosophy that Hangeul consonants should reflect the physical shape of the vocal organs.
*   **Stroke-adding Principle:** The method of creating new consonants by adding a line to a basic shape to represent a stronger version of that sound.
*   **Syllable Assembly:** The process of combining consonants and vowels into a single square-shaped block to form a syllable.
*   **Upright Vowels:** Vowels with a vertical orientation (e.g., ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅣ) that are positioned to the right of the consonant.
