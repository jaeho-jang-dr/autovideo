Making Syllables with 'ㅇ' and Vowels



Hello everyone! Today, we're going to learn about a very special friend in Korean, the 'ㅇ' (ieung). When this 'ㅇ' comes at the beginning of a word, it makes no sound. It's like wearing an invisible cloak! But without this 'ㅇ', vowels alone cannot form a character. So, shall we look together at how 'ㅇ' and vowels meet to become wonderful characters?

First is '아' (a). 'ㅇ' and 'ㅏ' meet to form '아'. '아' feels like opening your mouth wide and saying 'Ah!' like a child calling their mom. In Korean, '아이' means 'child'.

Next is '어' (eo). 'ㅇ' and 'ㅓ' meet to form '어'. '어' feels like opening your mouth slightly less than '아' and making a sound from the back of your throat. A word starting with '어' is '어깨' (eokkae), meaning 'shoulder'.

'오' (o) is the sound formed by 'ㅇ' and 'ㅗ'. You just need to round your lips and make an 'Oh~' sound. Do you know the word '오리' (ori)? A cute 'duck' is called '오리' in Korean.

This time, it's '우' (u). 'ㅇ' and 'ㅜ' meet to form '우'. Try to pronounce it by protruding your lips and saying 'Woo~'. The 'milk' we drink every day, '우유' (uyu), also starts with '우'. It's really easy!

'으' (eu) is the sound formed by 'ㅇ' and 'ㅡ'. Slightly open your lips sideways and make an 'Eu~' sound, relaxing your mouth. This pronunciation can be a bit challenging for Korean learners. The word '은행' (eunhaeng), meaning 'bank', also starts with '으'.

Next is '이' (i). 'ㅇ' and 'ㅣ' meet to form '이'. You just need to slightly pull your lips to the sides and say 'Ee~'. '이' (tooth) in our body or the number '이' (two) both use this character.

'애' (ae) is the sound formed by 'ㅇ' and 'ㅐ'. It's similar to '에', but try opening your mouth a bit wider and saying 'Ae~'. The English word 'apple' is called '애플' (aepeul) in Korean, and that's when '애' is used.

Finally, it's '에' (e). 'ㅇ' and 'ㅔ' meet to form '에'. Similar to '애', but for '에', you open your mouth less and comfortably make an 'E~' sound. The 'air conditioner' that keeps us cool in summer, '에어컨' (eeokon), also starts with '에'.

Today, we learned how the silent 'ㅇ' and various vowels combine to create wonderful Korean syllables. Now you can confidently read and write all these characters: '아, 어, 오, 우, 으, 이, 애, 에'! Consistent practice will greatly improve your Korean skills. Let's enjoy learning Korean again next time!
