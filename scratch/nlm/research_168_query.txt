Authoritative teaching references and primary sources for a complete 24-week Korean-as-a-foreign-language (KFL) curriculum for adult learners from absolute beginner to advanced. Cover comprehensively: (1) History and design of Hangeul — King Sejong the Great, Hunminjeongeum (created 1443, promulgated 1446), the featural alphabet principle, consonant shapes modeled on the speech organs and vowel shapes based on cheon-ji-in (heaven, earth, human); (2) Korean phonology — basic consonants g/n/d/r/m/b/s/ng/j (ㄱㄴㄷㄹㅁㅂㅅㅇㅈ), aspirated consonants ch/k/t/p/h (ㅊㅋㅌㅍㅎ), tense double consonants kk/tt/pp/ss/jj (ㄲㄸㅃㅆㅉ), simple vowels, double vowels and diphthongs, semivowel glides; (3) batchim final consonants and the seven representative final sounds; (4) Korean sound-change rules — liaison (yeoneum), nasalization, palatalization, assimilation, tensification; (5) syllable-block composition (moa-sseugi); (6) beginner survival Korean — greetings, self-introduction, native vs Sino-Korean numbers, dates and time; (7) intermediate everyday Korean — neighborhood and locations, directions and wayfinding, shopping and asking prices, ordering food and dining etiquette, public transportation and subway transfers, daily-routine action verbs, weather and the four seasons, hobbies and frequency adverbs; (8) advanced Korean — speech levels and honorifics (jondaetmal vs banmal), emotions and emotional nuance, expressing opinions and logical argument, emergencies and troubleshooting, describing people and personality, travel experiences and future plans, social planning and agreements; (9) TOPIK proficiency-level alignment, Revised Romanization of Korean, and established Korean-language teaching pedagogy and frameworks for foreign learners.
