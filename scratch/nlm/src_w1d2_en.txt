First Words from Vowels



Hello! Last time we learned the eight simple vowels. Today, let's learn some very first words made with those vowels alone.

Our first word, 'ai' — it means a child. It's made of just two vowels, 'a' and 'i', with no batchim. Say it with me: ai.

Second, 'i'. A single 'i' — lips pulled wide — is already a word. It means 'tooth'.

Third, 'oi' — a crunchy green cucumber. Again two vowels, 'o' and 'i'. Round your lips, then pull them wide: oi.

Fourth, 'uyu' — sweet white milk. Both 'u' and 'yu' push the lips forward and round. Slowly now: uyu.

Fifth, 'o'. The very sound you make when surprised — 'oh!' — is itself a word. Round your lips: o.

Sixth, 'au' — a warm word for a younger sibling. Open wide for 'a', then round for 'u': au.

Let's gather today's six words: ai, i, oi, uyu, o, au. Every one is made of vowels alone, with no consonants. Easy, right?

Practice today's words clearly in front of a mirror. Next time, we'll add consonants to make even more words. See you soon!
