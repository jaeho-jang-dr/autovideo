## I. Key Concepts and Theoretical Foundations

### 1. The Hangeul "Moasseugi" (Block) System
Hangeul is an alphabetic system that is written in "blocks" representing individual syllables. Every syllable block follows strict geometric rules:
*   **The Vowel Rule:** Position "2" in a block is **always** a vowel.
*   **The Consonant Rule:** Positions "1" (Initial), "3" (Final/Batchim), and "4" (Double Batchim) are **always** consonants.
*   **Alignment:** 
    *   **Vertical Vowels (ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅣ):** Placed to the right of the initial consonant.
    *   **Horizontal Vowels (ㅗ, ㅜ, ㅡ):** Placed below the initial consonant.
    *   **L-Shaped/Complex Vowels (ㅘ, ㅝ, ㅟ):** Wrap around the consonant (below and to the right).

### 2. Vowel Classification and Harmony
Korean vowels are categorized based on their phonetic qualities and philosophical origins (Cheon-Ji-In: Heaven, Earth, Man).

| Category | Vowels | Color Coding (Pedagogical Suggestion) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yang (Bright/Positive)** | ㅏ, ㅗ, ㅐ | Warm Colors (Red, Orange, Yellow) |
| **Yin (Dark/Negative)** | ㅓ, ㅜ, ㅔ | Cool Colors (Blue, Navy, Purple) |
| **Neutral (Man)** | ㅣ | Neutral/Translucent (White, Grey) |

**Vowel Harmony Rule:** Positive vowels generally combine with other positive vowels (e.g., ㅗ + ㅏ = ㅘ), while negative vowels combine with other negatives (e.g., ㅜ + ㅓ = ㅝ). The neutral vowel 'ㅣ' acts as a bridge and can combine with both.

### 3. Consonant Articulation and Types
Consonants are classified by the point of articulation and the intensity of the air release.

*   **Point of Articulation:** Lips (ㅁ, ㅂ, ㅍ), Teeth/Alveolar (ㄴ, ㄷ, ㄹ, ㅅ), Hard Palate (ㅈ, ㅊ), Soft Palate (ㄱ, ㅋ), and Throat (ㅇ, ㅎ).
*   **Phonetic Intensity:**
    *   **Plain (Pyeong-eum):** ㅂ, ㄷ, ㄱ, ㅅ, ㅈ (Relaxed).
    *   **Aspirated (Gyeok-eum):** ㅍ, ㅌ, ㅋ, ㅊ (Strong burst of air).
    *   **Tense (Gyeong-eum):** ㅃ, ㄸ, ㄲ, ㅆ, ㅉ (Hard/tightened vocal organs).

### 4. Phonological Rules (Sound Changes)
*   **Batchim (Final Consonants):** Although there are many consonant letters, only 7 sounds are realized in the final position: [ㄱ, ㄴ, ㄷ, ㄹ, ㅁ, ㅂ, ㅇ].
*   **Nasalization:** Occurs when stops (ㄱ, ㄷ, ㅂ) meet nasals (ㄴ, ㅁ), changing to [ㅇ, ㄴ, ㅁ].
*   **Liquidization:** When ㄴ meets ㄹ, it changes to the [ㄹ] sound (e.g., *Silla* -> [Silla]).
*   **Palatalization:** When ㄷ or ㅌ meets the vowel ㅣ, they change to [ㅈ] and [ㅊ].

## II. Educational Curriculum and Methodology

The proposed 12-week curriculum is divided into three proficiency levels, integrating cultural elements and interactive tools.

### 1. Level-Based Objectives
*   **Beginner:** Survival Korean (shopping, ordering, directions). Focus on "Phonics" and basic syllable assembly.
*   **Intermediate:** Familiar social topics (workplace, K-culture, social media). Focus on fluency and advanced phonological rules.
*   **Advanced:** Professional topics (economy, history, climate change, news). Focus on logical reasoning and media literacy.

### 2. Multi-Sensory Teaching (The Lego Method)
To help non-native speakers overcome the cognitive load of "Moasseugi," the sources suggest using a **digital block system** or **Lego bricks**:
*   **Visual:** Color coding for Yin/Yang vowels to prevent illegal combinations (e.g., ㅗ and ㅓ will repel each other like magnets).
*   **Tactile/Interactive:** A "Drag & Drop" interface where blocks "snap" into place in an Elkonin Box (syllable container).
*   **Auditory:** Immediate audio feedback for correct/incorrect assembly.

### 3. Motivation and Rewards

## III. Short-Answer Practice Quiz

**Q1: What is the primary rule regarding Position "2" in a Korean syllable block?**
> **Answer:** It is always a vowel.

**Q2: Describe the difference in placement between a vertical vowel (e.g., ㅏ) and a horizontal vowel (e.g., ㅗ).**
> **Answer:** Vertical vowels are placed to the right of the initial consonant; horizontal vowels are placed directly below the initial consonant.

**Q3: How many representative sounds can be used in the "Batchim" (final consonant) position?**
> **Answer:** Seven (ㄱ, ㄴ, ㄷ, ㄹ, ㅁ, ㅂ, ㅇ).

**Q4: In the "Hangul Block Master" game, what visual effect occurs when a user tries to combine a Yang vowel (orange) with a Yin vowel (blue)?**
> **Answer:** A "repulsion" animation occurs where the blocks shake and bounce back, indicating they cannot be combined due to vowel harmony rules.

**Q5: When should a learner use the ending "예요" versus "이에요" when introducing themselves?**
> **Answer:** "예요" is used when the preceding noun ends in a vowel (no batchim); "이에요" is used when the noun ends in a consonant (has batchim).

## V. Glossary of Important Terms

*   **Batchim (받침):** The final consonant(s) in a syllable block, located at the bottom.
*   **Cheon-Ji-In (천지인):** The philosophical principle of "Heaven, Earth, and Man" used to create the base vowels in Hangeul.
*   **Elkonin Box:** A digital "syllable box" used in the Hangul Block Master game to guide the placement of consonants and vowels.
*   **Fortition (Den-so-ri de-gi):** A phonological process where a plain consonant changes into a tense (double) consonant sound.
*   **Gyeok-eum (거센소리):** Aspirated consonants characterized by a strong burst of air (ㅋ, ㅌ, ㅍ, ㅊ).
*   **Moasseugi (모아쓰기):** The practice of grouping individual letters into a syllabic block rather than writing them in a linear sequence.
*   **Standard Korean (Pyojun-eo):** The modern Seoul dialect used by educated people, serving as the basis for national language norms.
*   **TOPIK / SKA:** National assessments (Test of Proficiency in Korean / Sejong Korean Assessment) used to certify a learner's language level.
*   **Vowel Harmony:** The rule where vowels are grouped by "energy" (Yin/Yang); similar energy vowels tend to cluster together in complex vowels or suffixes.
