## Part 1: Key Concepts and Structural Principles
### 1. The Syllable Block System
Unlike the linear alphabet of English, Hangeul is written in "blocks" that represent a single syllable. Every syllable block must follow specific geometric rules:
*   **The Vowel Rule:** Position "2" in any block is **ALWAYS** a vowel.
*   **The Consonant Rule:** Positions "1" and "3" (and sometimes "4") are **ALWAYS** consonants.
*   **Block Orientation:**
    *   **Vertical Vowels (ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅣ):** The vowel is placed to the right of the initial consonant.
    *   **Horizontal Vowels (ㅗ, ㅜ, ㅡ):** The vowel is placed below the initial consonant.
    *   **Final Consonants (Batchim):** The final consonant (Position 3) is always placed at the very bottom of the block.
### 2. Vowel Harmony and Philosophy (Cheon-ji-in)
The Korean vowel system is rooted in the Eastern philosophical principle of **Cheon-ji-in** (Sky, Earth, and Man):
*   **Components:**
    *   **Sky ( • ):** Represented by the short strokes/dots in vowels.
    *   **Earth (ㅡ):** The horizontal line, representing a flat surface.
    *   **Man (ㅣ):** The vertical line, representing a standing person.
*   **Vowel Harmony (Yin and Yang):**
    *   **Yang Vowels (ㅏ, ㅗ, ㅐ):** Bright and warm energy. Represented in learning materials by warm colors (Red, Orange, Yellow).
    *   **Yin Vowels (ㅓ, ㅜ, ㅔ):** Deep and dark energy. Represented by cool colors (Blue, Navy, Purple).
    *   **Neutral Vowel (ㅣ):** Can combine with both Yin and Yang. Represented by achromatic or transparent colors (White, Light Gray).
### 3. Consonant Classification
Consonants are classified by their articulation point and the strength of the air released:
*   **Plain (Lax):** ㄱ, ㄴ, ㄷ, ㄹ, ㅁ, ㅂ, ㅅ, ㅇ, ㅈ, ㅎ.
*   **Aspirated (Strong Air):** ㅋ, ㅌ, ㅍ, ㅊ. These usually have an extra stroke compared to their plain counterparts.
*   **Tense (Glottalized):** ㄲ, ㄸ, ㅃ, ㅆ, ㅉ. These are doubled versions of the plain consonants, requiring muscle tension in the throat.
