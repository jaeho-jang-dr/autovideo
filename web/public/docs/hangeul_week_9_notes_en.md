## I. Key Concepts
### 1. Hangul Structural Principles: Moasseugi (Syllable Blocking)
Hangul is an alphabetic system written in "blocks" that represent individual syllables. Unlike the linear arrangement of the Latin alphabet, Hangul uses a three-dimensional assembly method.
*   **Syllable Components:** A syllable block consists of a starting consonant (Initial/1), a vowel (Medial/2), and an optional final consonant (Final/3 or 4).
*   **Vowel Alignment:**
    *   **Vertical Vowels (ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅣ, etc.):** Placed to the **right** of the initial consonant.
    *   **Horizontal Vowels (ㅗ, ㅜ, ㅡ, etc.):** Placed **below** the initial consonant.
    *   **L-Shaped/Mixed Vowels (ㅘ, ㅝ, ㅟ, etc.):** These combine horizontal and vertical elements, wrapping around the initial consonant (below and to the right).
*   **Final Consonants (Batchim):** These are always positioned at the very **bottom** of the syllable block.
*   **Writing Direction:** Blocks and the characters within them follow the rule of **Left to Right** and **Top to Bottom**.
### 2. Vowel Harmony and Color Coding
Hangul vowels are rooted in Eastern philosophy (Cheon-Ji-In: Heaven, Earth, Human). Learning these through color-coded blocks helps visualize "Vowel Harmony."
| Vowel Type | Symbolism | Representative Vowels | Color Coding | Interaction Logic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yang (Positive)** | Bright/Active energy | ㅏ, ㅗ, ㅐ, ㅒ | **Warm Colors** (Red, Orange, Yellow) | Attract other Yang vowels to form compounds (e.g., ㅗ + ㅏ = ㅘ). |
| **Yin (Negative)** | Dark/Heavy energy | ㅓ, ㅜ, ㅔ, ㅖ | **Cold Colors** (Blue, Navy, Purple) | Attract other Yin vowels (e.g., ㅜ + ㅓ = ㅝ). |
| **Neutral** | Human (Mediator) | ㅣ | **Neutral/Transparent** (White, Light Gray) | Can combine with both Yang and Yin vowels without conflict. |
### 3. Interactive Gamification: "Hangul Block Master"
The pedagogical approach uses digital "Elkonin Boxes" (syllable boxes) to teach the scientific principles of Hangul through a multi-sensory experience.
*   **Visual Feedback:** Uses "Snapping" effects for correct placements and "Bouncing/Repelling" animations for incorrect combinations (e.g., trying to pair a Yang vowel with a Yin vowel).
