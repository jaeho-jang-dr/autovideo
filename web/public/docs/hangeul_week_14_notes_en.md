## I. Structural Principles of Hangeul
### 1. The Syllable Block (Moasseugi)
Hangeul is unique because it is written in "blocks" representing individual syllables rather than a linear sequence of letters. Each block must contain at least one consonant and one vowel.
**Basic Block Rules:**
*   **Vowel Position:** A vowel (represented as position "2" in educational models) is the core of every block.
*   **Consonant Position:** Consonants occupy positions "1" (initial), "3" (final/batchim), and sometimes "4" (double final).
*   **Vertical vs. Horizontal Vowels:**
    *   **Vertical Vowels (ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅣ, etc.):** Placed to the **right** of the initial consonant.
    *   **Horizontal Vowels (ㅗ, ㅜ, ㅡ, etc.):** Placed **below** the initial consonant.
### 2. The Yin-Yang and Neutral Vowel System
Based on Eastern philosophy (Cheon-Ji-In), vowels are categorized by their "energy" and visual properties, which dictate how they combine in "Vowel Harmony."
| Vowel Category | Vowels | Energy/Feeling | Visualization (Lego Concept) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yang (Positive)** | ㅏ, ㅗ, ㅐ | Bright, light, warm | Warm colors (Red, Orange, Yellow) |
| **Yin (Negative)** | ㅓ, ㅜ, ㅔ | Dark, heavy, cool | Cool colors (Blue, Navy, Purple) |
| **Neutral** | ㅣ | Balanced (Human) | Translucent or Greyscale (White, Grey) |

## II. Interactive Pedagogy: Hangeul Block Master
To help learners overcome the cognitive load of syllable assembly, the source proposes a "Multi-Sensory Teaching Method" using an interactive web game.
### 1. UI/UX Features
*   **Elkonin Box (Syllable Box):** An empty frame where learners drag and drop letter blocks. The guide lines change dynamically based on the vowel type (vertical, horizontal, or L-shaped).
*   **Snap & Magnet Effects:** Correct blocks "snap" into place with a xylophone sound. Vowels of the same energy (Yang-Yang) are attracted to each other like magnets.
*   **Bouncing/Repulsion Effect:** If a learner attempts an incorrect combination (e.g., pairing a Yang vowel with a Yin vowel), the blocks vibrate and "bounce" back to the palette.
