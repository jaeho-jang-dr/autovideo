## I. Key Concepts and Linguistic Principles
### 1. Hangeul Composition and the "Cheon-Ji-In" Principle
Hangeul's vowel system is rooted in Eastern philosophy, representing three primary elements:
*   **Cheon (天 - Sky):** Represented by a dot (now a short stroke).
*   **Ji (地 - Earth):** Represented by the horizontal line 'ㅡ'.
*   **In (人 - Human):** Represented by the vertical line 'ㅣ'.
The vowel 'ㅣ' is considered neutral, symbolizing a human standing between sky and earth. It can combine freely with both "Positive" (Yang) and "Negative" (Eum) vowels without visual or phonetic conflict.
### 2. Moasseugi (Syllable Block Construction)
Unlike linear alphabets (like the Roman alphabet), Hangeul is written in "blocks" where each block represents exactly one syllable.
*   **Position 1 & 3 (and 4):** Always consonants (Initial/Choseong and Final/Jongseong).
*   **Position 2:** Always a vowel (Medial/Jungseong).
**Block Placement Rules:**
| Vowel Type | Vowel Examples | Placement Logic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vertical** | ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅣ, ㅐ, ㅔ | Placed to the **right** of the initial consonant. |
| **Horizontal** | ㅗ, ㅜ, ㅡ | Placed **directly below** the initial consonant. |
| **Complex (L-Shaped)** | ㅘ, ㅝ, ㅟ, ㅙ | Combines both: one part below and one part to the right of the consonant. |
### 3. Vowel Harmony (Eum-Yang)
Vowels are categorized by their "energy" and sound quality, which dictates how they combine in complex (double) vowels:
*   **Positive (Yang) Vowels:** Bright and crisp sounds (e.g., ㅏ, ㅗ). Color-coded in warm tones (red, orange, yellow).
*   **Negative (Eum) Vowels:** Dark and heavy sounds (e.g., ㅓ, ㅜ). Color-coded in cool tones (blue, navy, purple).
*   **Neutral Vowels:** The 'ㅣ' vowel is neutral and represented by achromatics (white, light gray) or translucency.
*   **The Harmony Rule:** Warm colors "magnetically" attract other warm colors (e.g., ㅗ + ㅏ = ㅘ), while cool colors attract cool colors (e.g., ㅜ + ㅓ = ㅝ). Mixing them (e.g., ㅗ + ㅓ) creates visual and linguistic "repulsion."
### 4. Phonetics and Pronunciation
*   **Final Consonants (Jongseong):** While many consonants can be written in the final position, there are only 7 representative sounds: **ㄱ, ㄴ, ㄷ, ㄹ, ㅁ, ㅂ, ㅇ**.
