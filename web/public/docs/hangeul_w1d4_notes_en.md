### 2. Distinguishing similar vowels

In Korean, there are vowels that sound similar and are easy to confuse. I'll teach you how to distinguish these vowels accurately.

### 3. ㅗ vs ㅜ (duck/milk)

The first pair is 'ㅗ' and 'ㅜ'. For 'ㅗ', round your lips to make an 'o' sound. For 'ㅜ', push your lips out further to make a 'u' sound. Shall we practice with '오리' (duck) and '우유' (milk)?

### 4. ㅐ vs ㅔ (dog/crab)

Next are 'ㅐ' and 'ㅔ'. For 'ㅐ', open your mouth wide for an 'ae' sound. For 'ㅔ', open your mouth slightly for an 'e' sound. Listen to '개' (dog) and '게' (crab).

### 5. ㅏ vs ㅓ (horse/head)

This time, it's 'ㅏ' and 'ㅓ'. For 'ㅏ', open your mouth wide for an 'a' sound. For 'ㅓ', open your mouth slightly and lower your jaw for an 'eo' sound. Can you distinguish '말' (horse) and '머리' (head)?

### 6. ㅡ vs ㅜ (fire/horn)

Finally, 'ㅡ' and 'ㅜ'. For 'ㅡ', pull your lips slightly to the sides to make an 'eu' sound. For 'ㅜ', round and push your lips out to make a 'u' sound. Practice with '불' (fire) and '뿔' (horn).

### 7. Key vowel pronunciation summary!

For 'ㅗ' and 'ㅜ', lip shape was key. For 'ㅐ' and 'ㅔ', mouth size. For 'ㅏ' and 'ㅓ', jaw position. For 'ㅡ' and 'ㅜ', lip tension was important.

### 8. Repetition is key!

Well done! Now you should be able to hear the subtle differences in each vowel pair, right? It's important to get used to them by listening repeatedly.
