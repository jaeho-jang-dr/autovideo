## 1. Structural and Phonetic Foundations of Hangeul
### Syllabic Block Construction
Hangeul is unique in its "syllable block" (Moasseugi) system. Unlike linear alphabets, Hangeul characters are assembled into geometric blocks representing a single syllable.
*   **Block Composition Rules:**
    *   A block consists of at least one consonant and one vowel.
    *   **Position 2** is always a vowel.
    *   **Positions 1, 3, and 4** are always consonants.
    *   **Vertical Vowels (ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅣ):** Positioned to the right of the initial consonant.
    *   **Horizontal Vowels (ㅗ, ㅜ, ㅡ):** Positioned directly below the initial consonant.
    *   **Batchim (Final Consonant):** Always placed at the bottom of the syllable block.
### Phonetic Classification
The Korean phonetic system distinguishes between vowels and consonants based on the obstruction of airflow.
| Type | Characteristics | Key Components |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vowels** | Produced without obstruction of airflow from the lungs. | 21 total (10 monophthongs, 11 diphthongs). |
| **Consonants** | Produced by the contact or proximity of vocal organs. | 19 total, categorized by articulation point (labial, dental, etc.). |
### Standard Pronunciation Rules
Standard pronunciation is vital for effective communication. Key phonological phenomena include:
*   **Assimilation (Donghwa):** One sound influences another (e.g., nasalization where stops become nasals before 'ㄴ' or 'ㅁ').
*   **Tensification (Doensori-doegi):** Plain stops become tensed (e.g., 'ㄱ' becoming 'ㄲ') following certain final consonants.
