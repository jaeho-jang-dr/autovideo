## Study Notes

### 1. First Words from Vowels

Hello! Last time we learned the eight simple vowels. Today, let's learn some very first words made with those vowels alone.

### 2. 아이 = a child

Our first word, 'a-i' — it means a child. It's made of just two vowels, 'a' and 'i', with no batchim. Say it with me: a-i.

### 3. 이 = a tooth

Second, 'i'. A single 'i' — lips pulled wide — is already a word. It means 'tooth'.

### 4. 오이 = a cucumber

Third, 'o-i' — a crunchy green cucumber. Again two vowels, 'o' and 'i'. Round your lips, then pull them wide: o-i.

### 5. 우유 = milk

Fourth, 'u-yu' — sweet white milk. Both 'u' and 'yu' push the lips forward and round. Slowly now: u-yu.

### 6. 오 = oh! / five

Fifth, 'o'. The very sound you make when surprised — 'oh!' — is itself a word. Round your lips: o.

### 7. 아우 = younger sibling

Sixth, 'a-u' — a warm word for a younger sibling. Open wide for 'a', then round for 'u': a-u.

### 8. Today's Words

Let's gather today's six words: a-i, i, o-i, u-yu, o, a-u. Every one is made of vowels alone, with no consonants. Easy, right?

### 9. Review & Next Time

Practice today's words clearly in front of a mirror. Next time, we'll add consonants to make even more words. See you soon!
