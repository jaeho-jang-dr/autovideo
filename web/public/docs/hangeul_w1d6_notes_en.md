# King Sejong's Love for the People and the Story of Hangeul

> drjay-ed · Hunminjeongeum daily lesson

## Study Notes

### 1. Let's begin the story of how Hangeul was born!

Hello! Today I'll tell you the story of how Hangeul, the special writing Koreans use every day, was born.

### 2. King Sejong, who loved his people

Hangeul was created by King Sejong, the fourth king of Joseon. More than anyone, he had a warm heart that loved his people.

### 3. Chinese characters were too hard

Back then, people had to use difficult Chinese characters. They were so complex that most people could neither read nor write.

### 4. People struggled without writing

Unable to read, people suffered injustice in silence, and their lives were very hard. King Sejong's heart ached for them.

### 5. "We need our own letters!"

"We need our own letters that anyone can learn easily!" So King Sejong resolved to create them himself.

### 6. Research at the Hall of Worthies

With the young, brilliant scholars of the Hall of Worthies, he studied sounds and letters day and night.

### 7. He never gave up despite opposition

Many officials objected, "We must not abandon the old characters!" But for his people, King Sejong never gave up.

### 8. The secret of vowels: Heaven, Earth, Human!

The Hangeul they created is wonderfully scientific. Its vowels were modeled on heaven, earth, and human — "Cheon-Ji-In."

### 9. · sky   ㅡ earth   ㅣ person

'·' means sky, 'ㅡ' means earth, 'ㅣ' means person. Combine just these three and you get every vowel, like ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅗ, ㅜ.

### 10. Consonants = shape of speech organs!

The consonants are even more amazing — shaped after speech organs like the lips, tongue, and throat. 'ㄱ' came from the tongue blocking the throat.

### 11. Hunminjeongeum proclaimed, 1446!

In 1446, King Sejong shared these letters with all his people under the name "Hunminjeongeum" — "the correct sounds to teach the people."

### 12. Rain gauge, sundial, water clock — Sejong's works

King Sejong did more than create letters — he made science tools like the rain gauge, sundial, and water clock, and worked for music, farming, and defense, all for his people.

### 13. Thank you, King Sejong!

It's all thanks to King Sejong that we can learn Korean so easily today. May Hangeul be loved around the world — see you next time!
