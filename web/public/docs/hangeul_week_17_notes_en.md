## Detailed Analysis of Key Themes
### 1. Structural Principles of Hangul and Syllable Formation
The Korean writing system is distinctively structured into "blocks" representing individual syllables, rather than a linear arrangement. 
*   **Syllable Block Rules:** Each block contains exactly one syllable. A block must always include a vowel (position 2) and can include between one to three consonants (positions 1, 3, and 4).
*   **Vowel-Driven Layout:** The physical orientation of the vowel determines the block's shape:
    *   **Vertical Vowels (ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅣ, etc.):** Consonants are placed to the left or bottom.
    *   **Horizontal Vowels (ㅗ, ㅜ, ㅡ, etc.):** Consonants are placed above or below.
*   **Batchim (Final Consonants):** The final consonant (종성) is always placed at the very bottom of the block. Standard pronunciation recognizes only seven representative final consonant sounds (ㄱ, ㄴ, ㄷ, ㄹ, ㅁ, ㅂ, ㅇ), regardless of the actual orthography.
### 2. Multi-Sensory Pedagogical Methodology: The "Hangul Block Master"
To reduce cognitive load for learners accustomed to linear alphabets, the source material proposes a "Hangul Block Master" interactive web game.
*   **Interactive UI/UX:** The system utilizes "Elkonin Boxes" (syllable boxes) with dynamic guidelines that shift based on whether the vowel is horizontal or vertical.
*   **Snap-and-Magnet Effects:** Digital blocks "snap" into place when correctly positioned, providing immediate haptic and visual feedback.
*   **Color-Coded Vowel Harmony:** Based on Eastern philosophy (Yin-Yang), vowels are categorized by "temperature":
    *   **Yang (Bright/Warm):** Red, orange, and yellow for vowels like ㅏ and ㅗ.
