Here's a fun experiment. Hold a piece of paper in front of your mouth and pronounce the sounds. The paper will move with 'aspirated consonants', but hardly move with 'tense consonants'. This experiment is a great way to visually demonstrate the biggest difference between 'aspirated' and 'tense consonants'. 'Aspirated consonants' expel air with a 'whoosh', making the paper move, while 'tense consonants' hold the air 'tightly' in the throat, so the paper barely moves. Try it yourself and feel the difference in sounds. Isn't this a truly interesting experiment?
